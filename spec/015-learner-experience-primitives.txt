Feature: Learner Experience Primitives

Authors: product manager, senior UI/UX designer.
Relationship to prior work: layered on top of `spec/014-senior-ui-ux-polish.txt`, which ships the page-level UI polish (collection CTA, lecture navigation wiring, active section state, selectable quiz). This spec is a sibling that defines the cross-cutting helpers and a small set of new page-level primitives that complete the learner journey. Where 014 already defines a primitive, 015 defers to 014 and references it; 015 is self-contained for new work and consolidates the P0/P1 list for plan generation.

## Product Summary

The Lecture Site Engine has a mature component vocabulary (22 `lecture-component` types) and a clean authoring model. The remaining gap is not "more component types." It is the small set of page-level primitives and cross-cutting helpers that make a rendered course feel like a designed learning product instead of a stack of equal-weight cards.

This spec bundles two priority tiers:

- P0: page-level primitives that are visible to every learner and either missing or under-built today.
- P1: cross-cutting helpers that turn authored content into a navigable, paced learning surface.

Every primitive in this spec either composes data the engine already has (progress, glossary terms, authored sections, assessments) or upgrades an existing component's interaction model. None of them require a new `lecture-component` type, a new authoring field, a new dependency, or a breaking change.

The primary product outcome is: a learner opening a collection, a lecture, or a long section can answer three questions in under five seconds — *what is this, where am I, what do I do next* — without scrolling, hovering, or guessing.

## Product Outcome

Learners should experience the rendered course as a guided, paced, navigable learning path:

- The collection landing answers *what is this course, how long is it, and what should I do next?* in the first viewport.
- Long lectures stay navigable because the active section is always visible and resumable.
- Quizzes feel like practice with feedback, not reveal cards.
- Resuming returns the learner to the exact section they left, not just the lecture.
- Reading time is honest, visible, and updated as sections complete.
- Vocabulary and assessment moments are findable as indexes, not buried in prose.
- Mobile reading is calm: actions are reachable, chrome is compact, and content arrives first.

Educators and reviewers should see authoring artifacts (glossary terms, assessments, instructor notes) surface as discoverable indexes in the rendered page and the review package, with no extra authoring work.

## Users And Jobs

Primary users:

- Learners reading a local or static lecture collection on desktop or mobile.
- Technical educators previewing course flow and printing handouts.
- Reviewers checking handoff readiness.
- AI coding agents using the rendered output as a quality target.

Jobs to be done:

- As a learner, I want one obvious action on the collection page so I never wonder whether to start or resume.
- As a learner, I want to resume to the exact section I left, not the top of the lecture.
- As a learner, I want long lectures to show me which section I am reading as I scroll.
- As a learner, I want quizzes to feel like real practice: I select, I check, I learn.
- As a learner, I want to know how long a lecture will take before I commit.
- As a learner, I want vocabulary I encountered earlier to be findable later.
- As a learner, I want assessments visible without dominating the page that is supposed to start my course.
- As an educator, I want every authored artifact to surface as an index in the rendered output, not require extra authoring.
- As a reviewer, I want the collection page to stay scannable without losing authoring signals.

## Problem

The current site is functional, but the learner journey has visible friction that the component vocabulary alone cannot fix:

- The collection landing has no single primary action; lecture titles are links, but there is no `Start course` / `Resume course` / `Continue course` affordance.
- Resume targets are coarse: a learner returns to a lecture, not a section.
- Lecture navigation is implemented as a component but is not rendered on collection-mode lecture pages.
- The learning-path navigation is a static card; it does not show the current section as the learner scrolls.
- The quiz is reveal-only; selecting an option before reveal does nothing.
- Reading time is fixed at the lecture level and is not shown in a consistent place.
- Glossary terms are rendered in place but never collected into an index, so learners cannot revisit them.
- The assessment index on the collection landing can crowd out the lecture list and the primary action, especially on mobile.
- Section completion controls are small and easy to miss on touch devices.

Each gap is small. Together they make the course feel like a stack of boxes rather than a guided product.

## Goals

P0 — page-level primitives:

- G1. Ship a `CollectionPrimaryAction` that derives a `Start`, `Resume`, or `Continue` label and target from local progress and renders as the dominant action on the collection landing.
- G2. Wire the existing `LectureNavigation` into `LecturePage` in collection mode, with a compact top affordance and a full bottom affordance.
- G3. Add active-section state to `LearningPathNavigation` for long lectures, using `aria-current="location"` and a non-color cue.
- G4. Upgrade `Quiz` to a `SelectableQuiz` that supports option selection, a `Check answer` action, and explicit feedback (correct/expected/explanation) without implying grading.

P1 — cross-cutting helpers:

- G5. Add a `ResumePrompt` that appears on a lecture page when there is a known resume target deeper in the lecture.
- G6. Add a `ReadingTimeEstimate` derived from authored section word counts, rendered next to lecture metadata and (optionally) in the learning-path nav.
- G7. Add an auto-generated `GlossaryIndex` built from authored `glossary_term` components, with a collapsible surface and anchor links.
- G8. Add an `AssessmentIndexDisclosure` that keeps the assessment index accessible but defers it on mobile and below the lecture list on desktop.

## Non-Goals

- No new `lecture-component` types. Primitives in this spec compose or upgrade existing data and components.
- No new authoring schema, no new frontmatter fields, no new component YAML.
- No new runtime dependency. Reading time and glossary index are derived in the build.
- No learner accounts, authentication, sync, or server-side state.
- No grading, scoring, mastery tracking, or assessment-linked progress rules.
- No analytics, dashboards, or educator reporting.
- No AI-generated feedback at runtime.
- No remote fetching of any kind during render.
- No breaking change to existing valid lecture templates, single-lecture mode, collection mode, or the review package.
- No new keyboard shortcut that conflicts with browser, OS, or screen reader defaults.

## Product Decisions

- Resume is best-effort local. A wrong-but-reasonable resume target is better than blocking the learner. Fall back to the top of the lecture.
- Reading time is approximate and labelled as such (`~12 min read`). It is not a contract.
- The glossary index is auto-generated. Authors do not maintain it.
- The assessment index is closed by default on mobile and below the lecture list on desktop, but is always reachable in one tap or one scroll.
- Active section state uses IntersectionObserver on the client. The first authored section is rendered as active on the server so there is no flash of unstyled state.
- Quiz selection state is local-only. It is never persisted, exported, or synced.
- The collection CTA always links to a valid route. If progress is corrupted or empty, fall back to `Start course` → first valid lecture.
- All primitives are independent. Any one can ship without the others.

## UX Designer Notes

Treat the rendered course as one calm, dense workspace. The page is not a marketing landing page and not a settings dashboard. The content is the strongest element; everything else either supports reading or orients the learner.

Experience principles:

- One primary action per surface. The collection page has one CTA. The lecture page has one resume target when applicable. The quiz has one check action.
- Hierarchy first, decoration last. Use typography, spacing, and grouping before adding more borders.
- Mobile is a first-class reading mode. Stack deliberately; do not just stack desktop cards.
- Feedback teaches. Quiz feedback explains reasoning, not only right or wrong.
- Indexes are for finding, not browsing. Collapse them by default on small screens.
- Reduced motion is respected everywhere: progress transitions, active-section cues, and quiz feedback.
- All stateful UI degrades to a readable static HTML with the same information.

Visual language:

- Primary CTA uses the strongest surface (filled, full-width on mobile, inline on desktop).
- Secondary actions use a divider row, not a card.
- Active section uses a left rule (3px), bold label, and `aria-current="location"`. Color is never the only cue.
- Quiz selected option uses a 2px ring and a check icon. Feedback uses a calm tint, not red/green shouting.
- Glossary index uses a compact list with anchor links and a small chevron disclosure.
- Assessment disclosure uses a single-line button with count, e.g. `Show 7 assessments`.

## Priority

### P0: Page-Level Primitives

#### A1. CollectionPrimaryAction

A single primary action on the collection landing, derived from local progress.

Behavior:

- No known section progress → label `Start course`, target = first valid lecture.
- A partially complete lecture with a known incomplete section → label `Resume course`, target = first incomplete section anchor in that lecture.
- Progress exists but no precise target is derivable (corrupted, empty, or all-complete) → label `Continue course` when at least one lecture is incomplete, else `Restart course` → first valid lecture.

Visual:

- Top of `CollectionLanding`, immediately after the title and description.
- Filled button, full-width on `< 480px`, inline on wider viewports.
- Above the compact progress summary, above the assessment index, above the lecture list.
- Lecture count, total duration, and validation pass count appear as compact metadata below the title, secondary to the CTA.

Accessibility:

- Single `<a>` styled as a primary button.
- Visible focus ring.
- `aria-label` mirrors the visible label and includes the target lecture title when known.
- Reduced motion respected (no hover scale).

#### A2. LectureNavigation Wiring

Render the existing `LectureNavigation` in `LecturePage` when collection context is present.

Behavior:

- First lecture: back to course, next lecture.
- Middle lecture: previous, back to course, next.
- Last lecture: previous, back to course.
- Top affordance: compact row directly below the lecture header, only when collection context exists. Contains `Back to course` and `Next lecture` (or `Previous` on the last lecture).
- Bottom affordance: full `LectureNavigation` after key takeaways and answer key.

Visual:

- Top affordance uses a compact divider row, not a card.
- Bottom affordance matches the existing `LectureNavigation` styling.
- 44px minimum tap target height on touch.

Accessibility:

- `<nav>` element with descriptive `aria-label` (`Course navigation`).
- All actions are normal anchors; no JavaScript required.
- Visible focus.

#### A3. Active Section State

Make the current section visible in `LearningPathNavigation` as the learner scrolls.

Behavior:

- Client-side `IntersectionObserver` tracks the topmost visible authored section.
- Server-render the first authored section as active so there is no flash on load.
- Update active state on scroll with a small debounce; do not cause layout shift.
- Use `aria-current="location"` on the active anchor.
- Provide a non-color cue: a left rule plus bold label, with a screen-reader-only `Current` suffix.

Visual:

- Active anchor has a 3px left rule in the accent color and bold label.
- Inactive anchors are muted but readable.
- No animation on the rule; respect reduced motion.

Accessibility:

- Anchors remain real links; no scroll-jacking.
- Active state is announced to assistive tech via `aria-current`.
- Falls back to a static "you are at the top" state when JavaScript is unavailable.

#### A4. SelectableQuiz

Upgrade `Quiz` to support option selection before reveal and to teach through feedback.

Behavior:

- Learner can select one option by clicking or pressing Enter/Space.
- Selected option shows a 2px ring and a check icon.
- Primary action label changes from `Show answer` to `Check answer` when a selection exists.
- After `Check answer`:
  - Selected option is marked correct or incorrect.
  - The expected option is identified with a separate, non-judgmental cue (`Expected answer`).
  - The explanation is revealed inline.
- If the learner reveals without selecting, the UI shows the expected option and explanation without scolding.
- Hidden state is never treated as secure; answers remain in static HTML, source, and review packages.

Visual:

- Calm feedback tint, not red/green shouting.
- Expected-answer cue uses a left rule, not a fill.
- Mobile: full-width options, single column.
- Print: shows the expected option and explanation with a clear label, identical to no-JS fallback.

Accessibility:

- Options are real `<button>` elements with `aria-pressed` or `aria-checked`.
- Feedback region uses `aria-live="polite"`.
- Keyboard navigation between options with arrow keys.
- Visible focus on the active option.

### P1: Cross-Cutting Helpers

#### B1. ResumePrompt

A small, dismissible prompt on a lecture page that points to the first incomplete section.

Behavior:

- Appears on a lecture page when the learner has progress in the current lecture and there is at least one incomplete authored section.
- Single line: `Continue from <Section title>` plus a primary action.
- Dismissal is per-session (sessionStorage), not persistent. A reload re-shows the prompt if the section is still incomplete.
- Hidden on print.

Visual:

- Compact banner directly below the lecture header, above progress and the learning-path nav.
- Single primary button, no card border; uses a divider row.
- Reduced motion respected.

Accessibility:

- `<aside>` or `<section>` with `aria-label="Resume lecture"`.
- Action is a real link with a section anchor.
- Visible focus.

#### B2. ReadingTimeEstimate

Per-lecture reading time, derived from authored section word counts.

Behavior:

- Estimate = total authored words / 200 wpm, rounded to the nearest minute, with a 1-minute floor.
- Rendered next to lecture metadata as `~N min read` with a tilde to signal approximation.
- Optional per-section estimate in the learning-path nav (e.g. `3 min`), if it does not crowd the section label.
- Updates only on rebuild; this is build-time data, not runtime.

Visual:

- Inline metadata, not a card.
- Same typographic weight as duration and level.
- No icon, no progress bar.

Accessibility:

- Plain text. No special ARIA needed.

#### B3. GlossaryIndex

An auto-generated, navigable index of all `glossary_term` components in the current lecture or collection.

Behavior:

- Built at render time from authored `glossary_term` components.
- Alphabetical by term, with aliases folded in.
- Each entry links to the term's anchor on the page.
- Collapsed by default on mobile, expanded by default on desktop.
- On the collection landing, the index spans the whole collection.

Visual:

- Compact list inside a single surface.
- Disclosure uses a small chevron; no card border on mobile.
- Term is bold; definition is muted and one-line truncated with full text on hover/focus.
- Print: included as an appendix after the lecture, before the answer key.

Accessibility:

- Disclosure is a real `<button>` with `aria-expanded`.
- List uses semantic `<dl>` or `<ul>` markup.
- Visible focus on each link.

#### B4. AssessmentIndexDisclosure

A collapsed-by-default surface for the assessment index, designed not to block the start journey.

Behavior:

- Mobile: collapsed by default with a single-line button `Show N assessments` / `Hide N assessments`.
- Desktop: appears below the lecture list, above any instructor notes; expanded by default, with a disclosure.
- The index itself is unchanged; only its placement and default state change.
- Assessment anchors still work directly.

Visual:

- Single-line disclosure button, no card border.
- Index list uses compact metadata (component type, lecture title) and a one-line prompt.
- No icons, no badges.

Accessibility:

- Real `<button>` with `aria-expanded` and `aria-controls`.
- List uses semantic markup.
- Visible focus.

## Recommended Experience Direction

### Collection Landing

Preferred first-screen order:

1. Eyebrow and course title.
2. Short description.
3. Compact metadata: lecture count, duration, reading time, level, audience.
4. `CollectionPrimaryAction`.
5. Compact progress summary.
6. Compact lecture list, with a `View all lectures` anchor if the list is long.
7. `AssessmentIndexDisclosure` (collapsed on mobile, expanded on desktop, below the list).
8. `GlossaryIndex` (collapsed on mobile, expanded on desktop, below assessments).

The page reads as a course dashboard, not a report page. Validation and pass/fail badges remain visible but are visually secondary to the primary action.

### Lecture Page

Preferred flow:

1. Lecture title, description, and compact metadata including `~N min read`.
2. `ResumePrompt` (when applicable, dismissible per session).
3. `LearningPathNavigation` with active section state.
4. Overview and objectives.
5. Authored sections.
6. Key takeaways.
7. `GlossaryIndex` for the lecture (collapsed on mobile, expanded on desktop).
8. Answer key appendix.
9. `LectureNavigation` (top affordance plus bottom full nav).

`CollectionPrimaryAction` is on the landing, not duplicated here. The lecture page's primary action is the reading itself; resume is a prompt, not a button bar.

### Mobile Reading

- Primary CTA is full-width and above the fold.
- Disclosure controls use a chevron and 44px tap target.
- Reading time is inline, not a separate card.
- The learning-path disclosure is visibly interactive with a chevron and a count: `Learning path · 7 sections`.

### Active Section

Desktop learning path:

- Active anchor has a 3px left rule, bold label, and `aria-current="location"`.
- Inactive anchors are muted.
- No color-only cues.

Mobile learning path:

- Same rules, applied inside the disclosure body.
- The disclosure header is not marked active; the active cue is inside the list.

### Quiz Interaction

Selected:

- 2px ring + check icon.
- `aria-pressed="true"`.

After check:

- Selected option: tinted background, label `Your answer`.
- Expected option: left rule, label `Expected answer`.
- Explanation: revealed inline, polite live region.
- Print: same content, same labels, no ring.

### Glossary Index

Desktop:

- Three-column grid when width allows, otherwise stacked.
- Term bold, definition one-line truncated.

Mobile:

- Collapsed disclosure at the top of the lecture content.
- Expanded list uses the same compact row.

## Accessibility Requirements

- All CTAs, disclosures, and quiz options are keyboard reachable.
- All interactive elements have a visible focus ring that meets 3:1 contrast.
- Active navigation state never relies on color alone (left rule + bold + `aria-current`).
- Disclosure controls use real `<button>` elements with `aria-expanded`.
- Quiz feedback uses an `aria-live="polite"` region.
- Reading time is plain text and is included in the printed handout.
- The `ResumePrompt` is announced as a region with a clear label.
- Reduced motion is respected for progress transitions, active-section updates, and quiz feedback.
- Tap targets on touch devices are at least 44px high.
- No new keyboard shortcut conflicts with browser, OS, or screen reader defaults.

## Responsive Requirements

- 390px: primary CTA visible near the top of the collection page; no horizontal overflow.
- 390px: lecture page first viewport shows title, resume prompt (if applicable), and a compact progress summary; the learning-path disclosure is closed by default.
- 768px: collection page fits the CTA, compact progress, and the start of the lecture list without scrolling past the fold.
- 1280px: side navigation does not overpower the content column; the active section is visible in the learning path.
- Long quiz options, lecture titles, glossary terms, and URLs wrap inside their containers.
- Disclosure surfaces do not introduce body-level horizontal overflow at any breakpoint.

## Functional Requirements

- Existing valid lecture templates remain valid.
- Existing collection routes continue to render from `lectures/`.
- Single-lecture mode continues to render from the active template path.
- Static export and review package behavior remain compatible.
- Local progress storage remains local-only.
- Resume targeting works without remote state.
- All primitives degrade to a useful static HTML with the same information when JavaScript is unavailable.
- No unsafe HTML rendering is introduced.
- No new runtime network dependency is introduced.
- The glossary index is deterministic and identical between the rendered page and the review package.
- Reading time is computed at build time and is identical between the rendered page and the review package.

## Success Metrics / Quality Gates

- On a fresh collection, a learner can identify what the course is and start it in under five seconds.
- On a partially completed course, a learner can resume to the exact section from the collection page in one click.
- On any collection lecture, a learner can reach the previous or next lecture without returning to the collection page.
- In a long lecture, a learner can identify the active section from the learning path at any scroll position.
- A learner can complete a quiz option, check it, and read the explanation without leaving the lecture page.
- Reading time is shown for every lecture and is within 20% of the actual reading time for authored material.
- The glossary index surfaces every authored term and links to the correct anchor.
- The assessment index is reachable in one tap on mobile and does not crowd the start action on desktop.
- Screenshots at 390px, 768px, and 1280px show no clipping, overlap, or body-level horizontal overflow.
- Print output remains useful for instructor review and includes reading time, glossary index, and full quiz answer guidance.
- Existing parser, validator, rendering, progress, collection, review-package, and component tests still pass or are updated for intentional behavior.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The collection landing renders a `CollectionPrimaryAction` whose label is `Start course`, `Resume course`, `Continue course`, or `Restart course` based on local progress, and whose target is a valid route or section anchor.
- The collection CTA appears above progress, the assessment index, and the lecture list.
- `LecturePage` in collection mode renders a top compact nav and a bottom full `LectureNavigation` with previous, back, and next as appropriate for the lecture's position.
- The learning path on long lectures shows an active-section state with a left rule, bold label, and `aria-current="location"`.
- The first authored section is rendered as active on the server, before any client-side observer runs.
- A learner can select a quiz option, press `Check answer`, and see their selection labeled, the expected answer labeled, and the explanation revealed.
- A learner who reveals a quiz without selecting still sees the expected answer and explanation, with no scolding language.
- A `ResumePrompt` appears on a lecture page when the learner has progress and at least one incomplete section, links to that section, and is dismissible per session.
- Reading time appears next to lecture metadata and is computed from authored section word counts.
- The glossary index lists every authored `glossary_term` in the current lecture (and the whole collection on the landing) and links to the correct anchor.
- The assessment index is collapsed by default on mobile and below the lecture list on desktop, with a disclosure that meets 44px tap target height.
- All primitives degrade to a useful static HTML when JavaScript is unavailable, and the same content appears in the review package.
- Existing parser, validator, rendering, progress, collection, review-package, and component tests still pass or are updated for intentional behavior.
- Verification includes browser screenshots for collection landing, short lecture, long/component-heavy lecture, mobile assessment interaction, and quiz selection → check feedback.

## Out Of Scope For 015

The following were considered and intentionally deferred; they are not part of this spec:

- New `lecture-component` types (`prereq_check`, `key_takeaway_card`, `try_it`, `figure`, `poll`).
- A `review-mode` toggle that exposes `instructor_note` and hidden content in the browser.
- Per-section learner notes persisted to localStorage.
- Progress export/import as JSON.
- Diagram fullscreen, zoom/pan, and download SVG.
- Time-based progress (estimated remaining reading time as the learner marks sections complete).
- Multi-device progress sync.

These items remain candidates for a future `spec/016` once 015 is verified and stable.
